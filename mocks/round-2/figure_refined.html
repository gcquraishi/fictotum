Henry VIII

1491—1547 / King of England
Verified Q38370
Biographical Summary

Henry VIII is perhaps the most recognizable monarch in English history, known principally for his six marriages and his role in the separation of the Church of England from the Roman Catholic Church. His reign was a period of radical change in the English constitution, expanding royal power and initiating the English Reformation.
