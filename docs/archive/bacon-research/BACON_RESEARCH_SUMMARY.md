## DATA MODELING INSIGHTS

### Successful Node Types Demonstrated
- `:HistoricalFigure` - Contemporary actors (Kevin Bacon, Derek Jacobi, etc.)
- `:HistoricalFigure` - Historical subjects (Francis Bacon painter, JFK, Claudius, etc.)
- `:HistoricalFigure` - Fictional characters (Prince Hamlet)
- `:MediaWork` - Films, TV series, documentaries

### Successful Relationship Types
- `:APPEARS_IN {role, character_type, sentiment, year}` - Actor in media
- `:COLLABORATED_WITH {film, year, director, roles}` - Direct co-starring
- `:SUBJECT_OF {media_type, year}` - Historical figure as subject of media
- `:DIRECTED` - Director of media work
- `:PORTRAYED` - Actor playing historical figure

### Properties That Proved Valuable
- `canonical_id` - Unique identifier for disambiguation
- `release_year` / `birth_year` - Temporal grounding
- `historical_period` - ERA contextual grouping
- `sentiment` - Character portrayal classification (Heroic/Villainous/Complex)
- `wikidata_id` - Cross-reference with external databases
- `awards` - Notable accolades and recognition

## IMPLICATIONS FOR CHRONOSGRAPH

### Strengths Demonstrated
1. **Period Drama Specialization:** Clear opportunity to identify actors specialized in historical roles (Derek Jacobi, etc.)
2. **Network Density:** Actor collaborations create rich interconnected networks
3. **Temporal Coherence:** Films and documentaries create clear time-based relationships
4. **Biographical Pathways:** Can track how historical figures are portrayed and discussed across decades

### Opportunities Identified
1. **Documentary Ecosystem Mapping:** Track commentators, interviewers, and participants in historical documentaries
2. **Genre Network Analysis:** Cluster actors by their participation in historical dramas, period pieces, biographical films
3. **Renaissance Philosopher Gap:** Identify underrepresented historical figures in cinema and propose documentation
4. **Hub Identification:** Find key actors/directors who serve as bridges between multiple historical narratives

### Limitations Encountered
1. **Renaissance Philosopher Absent:** Major historical figure (Francis Bacon 1561-1626) has minimal cinematic coverage
2. **Indirect Connections:** Some potential paths require unverified collaborations
3. **Documentary Participant Data:** Less structured documentation of who appears in documentaries vs. scripted films
4. **Actor Disambiguation:** Multiple historical figures with same name (both Francis Bacons) requires careful canonical_id management
